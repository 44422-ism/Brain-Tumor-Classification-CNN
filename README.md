Problem statement:
Brain tumors are life-threatening conditions requiring early detection for effective treatment. Manual MRI analysis is time-consuming, prone to human error, and often inaccessible in resource-limited regions. Automated classification using AI can assist radiologists by quickly identifying tumor presence and type, improving diagnostic accuracy, enabling timely interventions, and providing critical information to patients and healthcare professionals for better clinical decision-making.
Domain and technology
Computer Vision for medical imaging in healthcare diagnostics
Frontend: Streamlit(python based interactive UI)
Backend: TensorFlow Lite (optimized AI inference)
SDGs; Good Health and Well-being (SDG 3)
Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure (SDG 9)
Reduced Inequalities (SDG 10)
Objective: 
To build an automated system to assist in detecting tumors and providing tumor type predictions.
Develop an AI-based system to detect brain tumors from MRI scans.
 Classify tumors into Glioma, Meningioma, and Pituitary type.
Provide quick, accurate, and accessible diagnostic support for healthcare professionals. 
Offer tumor-related information and hospital recommendations to patients.
